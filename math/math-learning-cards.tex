            $\vec{v} =
                \begin{pNiceMatrix}
                    a_1 \\ a_2 \\ \vdots \\ a_n
                \end{pNiceMatrix}
                \neq
                \begin{pNiceMatrix}
                    \CodeBefore
                    \cellcolor[HTML]{FFFF88}{1-1,2-1}
                    \Body
                    a_2 \\ a_1 \\ \vdots \\ a_n
                \end{pNiceMatrix}$

            &  n-dimensional vector. In broad terms, vectors are things one can add and linear functions are functions of vectors that respect vector addition \textsuperscript{\cite[][p.12]{cherneyLinearAlgebra2013}}. \par Order of components matters. \\
        \end{tcolorbox}


        \tcbsubtitle{Vector Calculations}
        \begin{tcolorbox}
            [
                skin=boxtablesimple,
                tabularray={
                        hline{2-Z} = {0.5pt,solid, gray},
                        columns={halign=l, valign=m},
                        column{1}={\dimexpr 0.3\dimexpr \maincolspan\thispostercolwidth \relax\relax, colsep=0pt},

                        rows={rowsep=4pt},
                    }
            ] 
            $\left\lvert \vec{x} \right\rvert = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^n x_i^2} = \sqrt{x_1^2 + x_2^2 + ... + x_n^2}$ 
            & Length (magnitude) of vector $\vec{x}$. \par Also called Euclidean norm or 2-norm \textsuperscript{\cite[][p.90]{cherneyLinearAlgebra2013}}. \\

            $\frac{\vec{x}}{\left\lvert \vec{x} \right\rvert} = 
            \begin{pNiceMatrix}
                    \frac{x_1}{\left\lvert \vec{x} \right\rvert} \\ \frac{x_2}{\left\lvert \vec{x} \right\rvert} \\ \vdots \\ \frac{x_n}{\left\lvert \vec{x} \right\rvert}
                \end{pNiceMatrix}$
            & Normalized vector (unit vector) in the same direction as $\vec{x}$ \textsuperscript{\cite[][p.262]{cherneyLinearAlgebra2013}}. \\

            $\vec{v} \cdot \vec{u} =
            \begin{pNiceMatrix}
                v_1 \\ v_2 \\ \vdots \\ v_n
            \end{pNiceMatrix}
            \cdot
            \begin{pNiceMatrix}
                u_1 \\ u_2 \\ \vdots \\ u_n
            \end{pNiceMatrix} = \sum_{i=1}^n v_i u_i = v_1 u_1 + v_2 u_2 + ... + v_n u_n$
            & Dot product (scalar product) of vectors $\vec{v}$ and $\vec{u}$ \textsuperscript{\cite[][p.89]{cherneyLinearAlgebra2013}}. The dot-product of 2 orthogonal vectors is 0 \textsuperscript{\cite[][p.30]{fischerMaschinellesLernenFuer2024}}. \\
